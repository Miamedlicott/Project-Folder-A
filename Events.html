Annual Events

Events that happen every year!



Summernats

Summernats, a portmanteau of "Summer" and "Nationals," is Australia's largest car festival, first held in 1987. The event takes place annually in January at Exhibition Park in Canberra (EPIC) and has become a significant cultural event for car enthusiasts across the country. It features a variety of activities, including burnout competitions, car parades, live music, and showcases of modified and classic vehicles.
Duration: The festival typically spans four days, with various events and competitions scheduled throughout.
Activities: Participants can enjoy a range of activities, including:

  • - Burnout competitions.
  • - Dyno testing.
  • - Car displays featuring custom and classic vehicles.
  • - Live music performances and entertainment.

Summernats not only showcases automotive culture but also contributes significantly to the local economy, drawing visitors from across Australia and beyond. The ACT Government has expressed strong support for the event, recognizing its importance to Canberra's tourism and community engagement.


National Multicultural Festival

The National Multicultural Festival is one of Australia's largest celebrations of cultural diversity, transforming Canberra into a vibrant hub of multiculturalism. This year, the festival will feature over 270 stalls and more than 200 performances, showcasing the rich cultural tapestry of Australia.
Location: The festival will take place in the city center and expand into Glebe Park, providing ample space for various activities and performances.
Activities: Attendees can expect a diverse lineup of cultural performances, workshops, and mouth-watering food stalls. Highlights include:

  • - Opening Ceremony: Featuring a Welcome to Country and performances from local artists.
  • - Cultural Workshops: Opportunities to learn traditional dances, cooking demonstrations, and more.
  • - Family-Friendly Activities: Special events for children, including face painting and musical performances.

The festival is not only a celebration of culture but also a significant contributor to the local economy. In previous years, it has attracted hundreds of thousands of visitors, providing a substantial economic boost to the region.
Entry to the festival is free, making it accessible for everyone to enjoy the rich cultural experiences on offer. However, visitors should budget for food and drinks from the various stalls.



Floriade

Floriade is a vibrant celebration of spring, attracting both locals and tourists to enjoy the beauty of flowers and engage in various educational and entertaining activities.
It's a perfect opportunity to experience Canberra's natural beauty and community spirit.
Location: The event is held at Commonwealth Park, located on Regatta Place in Canberra. It is easily accessible, just a 15-minute walk from the Light Rail stop.
Activities: Visitors can expect a range of activities, including:
Workshops: Hands-on horticultural workshops focusing on sustainable gardening and plant biology.
Night Fest: An after-dark festival featuring live music, food stalls, and illuminated garden displays.
Performances: Cultural celebrations, orchestral performances, and kids' shows will be part of the program.
Entry: General admission to Floriade is free, with no need for registration. However, some workshops and special events may require tickets.



Enlighten Festival

The Enlighten Festival in Canberra is an annual celebration of light, art, and culture that transforms the city into a vibrant playground of creativity.
Duration: The festival runs from February 27 to March 9 each year.
Activities: It features large-scale architectural projections, live music, performances, and food and drink vendors across the National Triangle and beyond.
Significance: The festival showcases iconic national institutions like the National Gallery of Australia and Parliament House, illuminated after dark with stunning projections.
Events: Highlights include the Lights! Canberra! Action! short film festival and various cultural activations.
This festival is a must-visit for both locals and tourists, offering a unique experience of Canberra's cultural institutions in a new light.
